    Know the Market

    Factors that affect buying & selling in the South Bay

    The Silicon Valley market has its own drivers. These are the forces I watch most closely for my clients.

    Tech job market

    Hiring, layoffs, RSU vesting and IPO activity at major employers directly shape buyer demand and budgets across the South Bay.

    Mortgage rates

    Every move in the 30-year fixed shifts monthly payments and purchasing power — critical in a high-price market.

    Inventory & lock-in

    Homeowners holding low pandemic-era mortgages are slow to sell, keeping supply tight and supporting prices.

    Schools & location

    Top-rated school boundaries, commute access and walkability command persistent premiums.

    Taxes & Prop 13

    Property tax basis, transfer taxes and capital-gains considerations factor heavily into seller timing.

    Seasonality

    Spring and early summer bring the most activity; timing your listing can affect both speed and price.

    Step by Step

    Your roadmap to buying or selling

    Know what's ahead. Here's how each journey unfolds in the South Bay — and where I'll guide you at every step.

    Buying a home

    1. Get pre-approved. Meet with a lender to confirm your budget and get a pre-approval letter — essential for competing in Silicon Valley.
    2. Define needs & budget. We pin down must-haves, cities, schools, and commute so we search with focus.
    3. Tour homes. We see listings quickly while they're fresh, and review disclosures and comps together.
    4. Make an offer. We craft competitive terms — price, contingencies, timeline — to win without overpaying.
    5. Escrow & inspections. Open escrow, complete inspections, and review reports before removing contingencies.
    6. Appraisal & loan. Your lender finalizes the appraisal and underwriting toward loan approval.
    7. Close & get keys. Sign, fund, record — and the home is yours.

    Selling a home

    1. Prep & price. We review comps and set a strategic price designed to attract strong offers.
    2. Stage & photograph. Repairs, staging, and professional media make your home stand out.
    3. List & market. We launch on the MLS with targeted online and local exposure.
    4. Review offers. We evaluate price and terms together and negotiate the strongest deal.
    5. Escrow & contingencies. Buyer completes inspections and removes contingencies on schedule.
    6. Appraisal. The buyer's lender confirms value to keep financing on track.
    7. Close. Sign, record, and hand over the keys — congratulations.

    Learn the Lingo

    Glossary & FAQ

    Plain-English answers to the terms and questions that come up most for South Bay buyers and sellers.

    Key terms

    Pre-approval
    A lender's written estimate of how much you can borrow, based on verified finances. Essential for a competitive offer.
    Escrow
    A neutral third party that holds funds and documents until all sale conditions are met.
    Contingency
    A condition in the contract (loan, appraisal, inspection) that must be satisfied or the buyer can cancel.
    Contingency removal
    When the buyer formally waives a contingency, signaling commitment and putting their deposit at risk.
    PITI
    Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance — the four parts of a typical monthly mortgage payment.
    Points
    An optional upfront fee (1 point = 1% of the loan) paid to lower your interest rate.
    Appraisal gap
    The difference when a home appraises below the offer price; the buyer may need to cover it in cash.
    Earnest money
    A good-faith deposit that shows you're serious; it's credited toward your purchase at closing.
    Prop 13
    California law capping property tax at ~1% of assessed value, reset to your purchase price when you buy.
    HOA
    Homeowners Association — collects dues for shared maintenance in many condos and townhomes.

    Frequently asked questions

    How much do I need for a down payment in the South Bay?

    Conventional loans often start at 5% down, but many Silicon Valley buyers put 20% down to strengthen their offer and avoid mortgage insurance. Low-down-payment and first-time-buyer programs also exist. The right amount depends on your goals, cash, and the home's price.

    How long does it take to buy a home?

    Once you're in contract, closing typically runs 30–60 days. Finding the right home can take days to months depending on inventory and your criteria.

    What is Proposition 13 and how does it affect my taxes?

    Prop 13 caps California property taxes at roughly 1% of assessed value plus local add-ons and limits annual increases to about 2% until the home changes hands. When you buy, it's reassessed to your purchase price — your new tax basis.

    Is it a good time to buy if mortgage rates are high?

    Higher rates raise payments but often mean less competition and more negotiating room. Many buyers purchase now and refinance later if rates fall. The best time depends on your finances and how long you'll stay.

    What does it cost to sell a home in California?

    Sellers typically pay commissions, a share of escrow and title fees, county transfer tax, and any agreed credits or repairs — commonly in the 6–8% of sale price range.
